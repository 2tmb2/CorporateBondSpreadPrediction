FEATURE GUIDE -- CorporateBondSpreadPrediction
==============================================
Target variable: BAA10Y -- Moody's Baa Corporate Bond Yield minus 10-Year Treasury yield.
A wider spread = more credit risk perceived by the market.

All engineered features are purely backward-looking (no look-ahead bias).
Leading NaN rows (from rolling windows) are dropped by the training mask in each model.


RAW DATASET COLUMNS
-------------------
BAA10Y          Target. Daily Baa-10Y spread (percentage points). Source: FRED BAA10Y.

DFF             Federal Funds Rate (daily). The overnight rate the Fed sets;
                drives short-term borrowing costs.

DGS10           10-Year US Treasury yield (daily). Benchmark long-term risk-free rate.

DGS2            2-Year US Treasury yield (daily). Short-term rate expectations.

DAAA            Moody's Aaa corporate bond yield (daily). Highest-quality corporate rate.

DBAA            Moody's Baa corporate bond yield (daily). Lowest investment-grade rate.
                BAA10Y = DBAA - DGS10.

VIXCLS          CBOE VIX volatility index (daily). Market's expectation of near-term equity
                volatility; often called the "fear gauge."

DCOILWTICO      WTI crude oil price (daily). Economic activity proxy; oil demand rises
                with growth.

SP500           S&P 500 closing price (daily). Broad US equity market level.

CPIAUCSL        Consumer Price Index (monthly, forward-filled). Headline inflation.
CPIAUCSL_days_since  Calendar days since last CPI release.

PPIACO          Producer Price Index (monthly, forward-filled). Upstream cost pressure.
PPIACO_days_since

PCEPI           PCE Price Index (monthly, forward-filled). Fed's preferred inflation measure.
PCEPI_days_since

UNRATE          Unemployment rate (monthly, forward-filled). Labour market slack.
UNRATE_days_since

GDPC1           Real GDP (quarterly, forward-filled). Broadest economic output measure.
GDPC1_days_since

INDPRO          Industrial production index (monthly, forward-filled). Factory/mine/utility
                output; leading cyclical indicator.
INDPRO_days_since

UMCSENT         University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment (monthly, forward-filled).
                Household confidence; leads consumption.
UMCSENT_days_since

TCU             Capacity utilization rate (monthly, forward-filled). Fraction of industrial
                capacity in use; high values precede investment and inflation.
TCU_days_since

NFCI            Chicago Fed National Financial Conditions Index (weekly, forward-filled).
                Composite of 105 financial variables. Positive = tighter than average.
NFCI_days_since

M1SL            M1 money stock (monthly, forward-filled). Adjusted for the May-2020
                redefinition that moved savings deposits into M1.
M1SL_days_since

M2SL            M2 money stock (monthly, forward-filled). Includes savings deposits,
                small time deposits, retail money funds.
M2SL_days_since


ENGINEERED FEATURES (from feature_engineering.py)
--------------------------------------------------

BAA10Y_roll21_mean
  21-day rolling mean of BAA10Y. Smoothed spread; removes day-to-day noise to reveal
  the underlying trend. A rising mean signals worsening credit conditions.

BAA10Y_roll21_std
  21-day rolling standard deviation of BAA10Y. Spread volatility over the past month.
  High std = unstable credit environment; often precedes directional moves.

BAA10Y_mom5
  BAA10Y - BAA10Y shifted 5 days. Short-term (one-week) momentum in the spread.
  Positive = spread widened recently; negative = tightened.

BAA10Y_mom21
  BAA10Y - BAA10Y shifted 21 days. Monthly momentum. Captures medium-term directional
  pressure on credit risk.

BAA10Y_accel
  BAA10Y_mom5 - BAA10Y_mom5 shifted 5 days. The second derivative of the spread.
  Positive = momentum is accelerating (spread widening faster); negative = decelerating
  or reversing. Useful for catching turning points.

BAA10Y_zscore252
  (BAA10Y - 252-day rolling mean) / 252-day rolling std. How many standard deviations
  the current spread is from its 1-year average. Strong positive values signal an
  overextended spread likely to mean-revert; strong negative values signal compression.

BAA10Y_RSI14
  14-day Relative Strength Index applied to BAA10Y. Ranges 0-100.
  Above 70 = spread overbought (may tighten); below 30 = oversold (may widen).
  Standard momentum oscillator adapted to the credit market.

yield_curve_slope
  DGS10 - DGS2. The 2s10s spread. Positive = normal upward-sloping curve;
  negative (inverted) = historically reliable recession warning and often precedes
  spread widening. One of the strongest macro predictors in the feature set.

yield_curve_chg21
  yield_curve_slope - yield_curve_slope shifted 21 days. Whether the yield curve is
  steepening or flattening over the past month. Rapid flattening often foreshadows
  credit stress even before inversion occurs.

VIX_roll21_mean
  21-day rolling mean of VIX. Smoothed fear level; filters spikes to show the
  underlying volatility regime.

VIX_mom21
  VIXCLS - VIXCLS shifted 21 days. Month-over-month change in fear. Rising VIX
  momentum typically correlates with spread widening.

VIX_spike
  VIXCLS / VIX_roll21_mean. How elevated today's VIX is relative to its own recent
  average. Values > 1.2 indicate an unusually fearful market day (regime shift signal).

SP500_mom21
  SP500 / SP500 shifted 21 days - 1. Equity market return over the past month.
  Rising equity prices generally coincide with spread tightening (risk-on); falling
  equity with spread widening (risk-off).

DFF_mom21
  DFF - DFF shifted 21 days. Change in the Fed funds rate over the past month.
  Rising DFF_mom21 = the Fed is tightening, which typically pressures credit spreads.

credit_tier_spread
  DBAA - DAAA. The spread between Baa and Aaa corporate yields. Measures within-
  credit-market quality differentiation. Widening = investors demanding more premium
  for taking on lower-quality credit risk (flight-to-quality within corporates).

credit_tier_chg21
  credit_tier_spread - credit_tier_spread shifted 21 days. Whether the quality gap
  between investment-grade tiers is growing or shrinking over the past month.

risk_on_off
  SP500_mom21 * (-BAA10Y_mom21). Cross-asset flow composite.
  Positive = equity rising AND spread falling simultaneously (classic risk-on).
  Negative = equity falling AND spread rising (classic risk-off / flight-to-safety).
  Near zero = markets are not moving in lockstep, suggesting mixed signals.

oil_mom21
  DCOILWTICO / DCOILWTICO shifted 21 days - 1. Oil price return over the past month.
  Rising oil reflects stronger demand / growth expectations; falling oil can signal
  recession fears, both of which affect credit spreads with a lag.

NFCI_trend
  NFCI - NFCI shifted 20 days. Four-week change in the Chicago Fed Financial
  Conditions Index. Positive = financial conditions tightening (higher NFCI is worse);
  negative = loosening. Captures near-term momentum in the broadest financial stress
  composite available at weekly frequency.

month_sin, month_cos
  Sine and cosine encoding of the calendar month (month_sin = sin(2*pi*month/12),
  month_cos = cos(2*pi*month/12)). Captures smooth annual seasonality in credit spreads
  without requiring dummy variables for each month.

time_idx
  (date - 1990-01-01).days / 365.25. Years elapsed since 1990-01-01 (e.g. 2024 ~ 34.0).
  Allows models to learn secular regime shifts -- e.g. pre-2008 credit conditions
  were structurally different from post-crisis ones. Without this, two observations
  with identical feature values but 20 years apart look the same to the model.
  Acts as a slow-moving background signal; should not dominate over economic features.
